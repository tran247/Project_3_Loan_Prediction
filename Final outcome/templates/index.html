The story behind the model


As a result of the mortgage crisis, many layers of fact checking have been added into the mortgage process by regulators and by Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac and FHA (the national authorities who finance the majority of mortgage loans in this country).

Nowdays, companies look at multiple metrics to ensure financial return when when providing customers with loans.Company wants to automate the loan eligibility process (real time) based on customer detail provided while filling online application form. These details include customers' professional, personal and financial situation.

Our strategy was to use metrics such as Gender, Marital Status, Education, Number of Dependents, Income, Loan Amount, Credit History and others to create a model that will automate the loan eligibility process (real time) based on the data we found in Kaggle.

The data has 615 rows and 13 columns and will provide us with enough taste of working on data sets from insurance companies, what challenges are faced, what strategies are used, which variables influence the outcome etc

The goal is to identify the customers segments, those are eligible for loan amount so that they can specifically target these customers

Classification report


A Classification report is used to measure the quality of predictions from a classification algorithm. How many predictions are True and how many are False.

The report shows the main classification metrics precision, recall and f1-score on a per-class basis. The metrics are calculated by using true and false positives, true and false negatives. Positive and negative in this case are generic names for the predicted approved and non-approval classes.

As you can notice in the heatmap above, Our model shows an average accuracy of 80% with important ability of the positive cases did we were able to catch as well as postive preidctions.
